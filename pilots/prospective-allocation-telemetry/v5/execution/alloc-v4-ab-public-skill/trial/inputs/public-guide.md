# Public guide: bounded evidence synthesis

**Status:** builder-authored internal procedure, not expert-approved benchmark guidance and not an exact rubric.

1. Inventory all evidence IDs before choosing a decision.
2. Separate four claim families: judge consistency, individual human/automated concordance, absolute readiness, and configured-system intervention evidence.
3. For each favorable claim, seek the strongest supplied counterevidence and preserve authority, scope, and caveat.
4. Treat the seven-run ablation as directional; do not turn bundled model/harness/integration evidence into a general causal claim.
5. Choose any allowed recommendation whose threshold, minimum experiment, reconsider condition, and change evidence are mutually consistent.
6. Verify matrix-to-memo citations and distinguish source-reported numbers from prospective program thresholds.

Lineage: steps 1, 3, 4, and 6 restate `public-task.md` and the public evidence rows. The four-family decomposition and final consistency pass are builder-authored, unvalidated design hypotheses. They add no hidden obligation; the task itself discloses all scored artifact requirements.
