# Prospective self-inspection repair instrument v2

This bounded building/validation slice advances charter objectives B/C. It tests the general, cross-domain hypothesis that information-specific repair support can outperform equal-budget retry without extra collateral damage. The roster CSV, service INI, and workflow SVG are independent **synthetic mechanism probes**, not domain commitments or evidence of professional validity.

The redesign responds directly to `reports/validation/2026-07-19-self-inspection-repair-v1-outcome-validity-audit.md`: v1 saturated at 10/10 repair passes over only memo and JSON templates. V2 preserves every v1/execution byte and prospectively adds three new artifact/workflow shapes, two defect strata per family, two independently implemented condition-blind observers, and five planned repetitions per task-condition-configured-system cell.

## Evidence and fairness

Each `sources/*-rules.json` file is the authoritative public source for its builder-authored synthetic family. Every task repeats the fair public basis, identifies authoritative native and derived render views, pins a transformation, and types endpoint and collateral outcomes. These sources are authoritative only for exact internal fixtures; they convey no expert or real-world authority.

`observer_a.py` uses native parsers; `observer_b.py` uses an independently written line/token projection. Agreement is mandatory. Disagreement yields `observer_invalid`, never an inferred score. Calibration plants false acceptance/rejection, alternate-valid-artifact, missing-view, transform-drift, invalid-artifact, and collateral-damage cases. The builder-only pass fraction must remain strictly between 0.15 and 0.85. Treatment outcomes may not be used to tune the freeze.

## Prospective design

Five conditions share tool, harness, configured system, provider, and budget. `retry_no_new_information` is the control. There are 150 planned assignments (6 tasks × 5 conditions × 5 repetitions), each with a frozen seed and zero attempts. The primary estimand is treatment-minus-retry repair pass rate averaged within family and then equally across families, with task-family-clustered uncertainty. Shape reversal or uncertainty spanning zero blocks a treatment claim. Secondary estimands are collateral preservation, tokens, and time.
