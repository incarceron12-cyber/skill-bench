# Source-at-state omission and substitution conformance

This bounded **internal synthetic calibration** converts the Bridge Evidence review's deletion-and-replay idea into an executable cross-domain matrix. It advances charter objectives B and C by testing reusable evidence-use machinery; procurement and incident response are unlike work shapes, not scope commitments.

## What is replayed

The frozen fixture contains an evidence-to-decision memo and an operational incident brief. Each has builder-authored terminal, transition, two substitute, stale-contradictory, distractor, and length-matched neutral-control sources. One equivalent substitute and the stale/neutral controls are held outside the baseline and exposed only by their declared intervention. Source contents, frozen prefixes, configured-system records, and every realized intervention context have SHA-256 identities.

Each shape executes 13 deterministic cells: baseline; two unchanged replays; transition omission; length-preserving neutral replacement; cue masking; single terminal omission; equivalent-source substitution; single-substitute omission; joint terminal/substitute omission; stale-contradictory substitution; distractor omission; and a missing-post-state invalid cell. Unchanged replays are a fail-closed noise canary. Invalid observations are not zero effects.

Effects remain a vector: final acceptance, next-operation validity, safety preservation, and effort. The first three are baseline minus variant; effort is variant minus baseline. No scalar aggregation is permitted. A planted procurement omission reduces effort while worsening final, next-operation, and safety outcomes, demonstrating why efficiency cannot compensate for harm. Single omission can be null while joint omission exposes source-set necessity.

## Provenance and claim boundary

Design evidence is the complete review `papers/agent-benchmarks/2026-07-17-bridge-evidence-causal-use-validity.md`, especially **Unique insight**, **Transferable design implications for skill-bench**, and **Test**. The review is grounded in immutable arXiv v1 PDF/text recorded there, but the paper released no row-level run package. This fixture therefore does not reproduce its experiment.

The replay supports only exact behavior of builder-authored deterministic records. It does **not** establish agent capability, professional validity, portable document utility, semantic mediation, population prevalence, cross-domain generalization, deployment readiness, or an external treatment effect. It reuses existing benchmark, validity, and metric concepts and adds no retrieval-specific schema.

## v0.2 staged-lineage continuation

The immutable-parent v0.2 continuation adds cross-stage conservation without changing
`fixture.json` or `replay-report.json`. It is grounded in the complete AutoSynthesis
review and tests the general hypothesis that endpoint proximity is a lossy observation,
not validation of a professional chain. Controlled-intervention and nonintervention
association synthesis are unlike mechanism cases, not benchmark scope commitments.

Ten frozen cases preserve protocol → search → screen → study-map → extraction →
transformation → analysis → report lineage. In each shape, the matrix includes a clean
control, duplicate reports incorrectly treated as independent, a verbatim number bound
to the wrong role, offsetting extraction errors that leave the endpoint unchanged, and
an authorized protocol difference whose endpoint and decision legitimately diverge.
The replay reports endpoint distance and decision agreement separately, diagnoses the
earliest failed stage, blocks all six close-endpoint cases with consequential breaks,
and accepts the two authorized divergences only when their authority locators remain.

This deterministic internal calibration does not establish statistical correctness,
agent capability, professional validity, population prevalence, cross-domain
generalization, an external treatment effect, or readiness. Its evidence is limited to
exact behavior over the frozen synthetic records and fail-closed mutations.
