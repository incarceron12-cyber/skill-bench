# Public analytical lifecycle guide

Keep these nodes separate in your output:

1. Bind the descriptive anomaly to its source.
2. State a falsifiable candidate without promoting plausibility to truth.
3. Preserve a materially different rival.
4. Choose the public test whose possible outcomes distinguish the candidate from the rival; an anomaly-confirming test may not be discriminating.
5. State the predicted discrimination before relying on the result.
6. Adopt relevant corroborating and contradictory records rather than narrating around them.
7. Update or reject hypotheses under the evidence; do not equate temporal order with causation.
8. Bound the conclusion to this source pack and preserve a concrete residual uncertainty.
9. Recommend only a reversible, authorized-by-evidence consequence or escalation; abstain from blame or sanction when cause is unresolved.

The harness executes the selected test independently after submission. This guide contains no expected answer, preferred test ID, hidden status, grader rationale, or authorization label.
