# Reference roles and criterion observability

The optional `task.reference_materials` and `check.reference_comparison` records keep exemplar-derived criteria from silently licensing execution, superiority, novelty, expert-equivalence, or readiness claims.

## Boundary

- A reference role is typed as `construction`, `grader_only`, or `participant_visible`; roles describe use, not truth or professional authority.
- A public reference marked `not_observed` must name a versioned exposure policy. Runtime hiding alone is not evidence of no prior, release, model, or search exposure.
- Each comparison declares a narrow relation and the observer's evidence types, sufficiency judgment, and locators. Execution comparisons require command-trace or execution-result evidence; report prose alone fails closed.
- `metric_improved_under_matched_protocol` and the deliberately broad `surpasses_reference` relation require a versioned baseline, matched protocol, and passed independent verification with evidence. The broad relation is retained only to make overclaiming explicit; authors should prefer narrower relations.

These records reuse source IDs, versioned component hashes, check provenance, and existing trial evidence. They do not establish capability or professional validity.

## Evidence basis and calibration scope

The design is derived from the full-paper and release audit at `papers/agent-benchmarks/2026-07-15-researchclawbench-scientific-rediscovery-validity.md`, especially **Unique insight**, **Limitations and validity threats**, and **Transferable benchmark-design implications**. The fixture is internal contract calibration only: its baseline, execution, and independent-verification locators are synthetic and do not assert a real performance improvement.
