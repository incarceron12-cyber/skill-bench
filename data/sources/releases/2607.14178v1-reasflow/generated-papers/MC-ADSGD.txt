1   Introduction
Decentralized stochastic optimization has become a standard tool for large-scale learning
when data and computation are distributed across many workers but a central parameter
server is either undesirable or unavailable. In such systems, each node only exchanges
messages with its neighbors according to an underlying communication graph. This ar-
chitecture improves robustness, alleviates the single-point bottleneck of centralized syn-
chronization, and naturally matches settings where data are generated and stored at the

edge. At the same time, local computation and network communication are tightly cou-
pled: aggressive local updates may accelerate descent, while insufficient mixing ampli-
fies disagreement across the network and slows global convergence.
    A large body of work has therefore focused on characterizing the trade-off between
optimization progress and consensus error for decentralized stochastic gradient meth-
ods. Classical decentralized SGD (often called D-SGD or D-PSGD) uses one consensus
step per iteration and achieves convergence guarantees that explicitly depend on the
spectral gap of the mixing matrix, the condition number of the objective, and the vari-
ance of the stochastic gradients [5, 14, 17]. Subsequent methods such as EXTRA, exact
diffusion, and gradient-tracking variants reduce steady-state bias and improve commu-
nication efficiency by better correcting the drift between local models and the network av-
erage [7, 11, 13, 18]. In parallel, accelerated methods and multi-consensus schemes have
demonstrated that several rounds of communication per iteration, possibly combined
with polynomial (Chebyshev-type) gossip, can substantially improve the dependence on
the spectral gap [2, 4, 12].
    Despite substantial progress on decentralized optimization, it remains useful to un-
derstand how accelerated optimization dynamics and accelerated communication prim-
itives interact in the stochastic strongly convex regime. In particular, one would like to
clarify how multiple communication rounds within each outer iteration affect the over-
all dependence on the condition number, the network spectral gap, the stochastic noise
level, and the residual terms induced by data heterogeneity.
    Motivated by this question, we propose Multi-Gossip Accelerated DSGD (MC-ADSGD),
a decentralized stochastic optimization algorithm that combines a Nesterov-type primal–
dual extrapolation with two calls to a fast gossip averaging operator (FGA) per outer it-
eration. The gossip depth R controls the number of communication rounds inside each
FGA call. In the parameter regime considered in our analysis, we further couple the mini-
batch size and the gossip depth through the choice B = R, so that increasing R improves
communication accuracy while also reducing gradient variance.
    Our focus in this paper is on presenting the proposed MC-ADSGD method and mak-
ing its convergence and communication-complexity implications explicit in the strongly
convex stochastic setting. Under standard smoothness, strong convexity, and bounded-
variance assumptions, we derive a Lyapunov-based convergence bound consisting of a
linearly decaying term together with stochastic and heterogeneity-dependent residual
terms. This bound leads to a communication-complexity consequence of order

2    Related Work
This section briefly reviews the most relevant literature on decentralized stochastic opti-
mization, multi-round and accelerated gossip, and communication-efficient accelerated
methods, and situates Multi-Gossip Accelerated DSGD within this landscape.

Decentralized stochastic gradient methods. Early decentralized stochastic gradient meth-
ods such as D-SGD and D-PSGD [5, 14] apply one consensus (or gossip) step per iteration
and establish convergence guarantees that explicitly depend on the spectral gap of the
underlying mixing matrix. These methods are simple and widely used, but their com-
munication complexity can be suboptimal on poorly connected graphs, and their steady-
state error typically reflects a nonvanishing disagreement component. Variants based on
EXTRA and exact diffusion [13, 17, 18] as well as gradient-tracking schemes such as DIG-
ing, NEXT, and related algorithms [7, 11, 16] mitigate this issue by tracking the global
gradient more accurately and correcting local drift. Many of these works operate either
in deterministic or diminishing-stepsize regimes; when stochastic gradients and constant
stepsizes are considered, the resulting steady-state bounds often make the dependence
on noise and network parameters only partially explicit.

Gradient tracking and exact diffusion. Gradient-tracking methods maintain auxiliary
variables that estimate the average gradient across the network and use them to drive
local updates toward the global optimum. In the deterministic setting, such methods
achieve exact convergence and, in some cases, are known to be optimal up to logarithmic
4                            ReasFlow Paper Copilot / CSIAM Trans. Appl. Math., x (20xx), pp. 1-25


factors in terms of the dependence on the condition number and the spectral gap [12].
In the stochastic strongly convex case, recent works have extended gradient tracking
and exact diffusion to handle gradient noise and non-identical data distributions across
nodes [10, 15]. Compared with these approaches, the proposed MC-ADSGD algorithm
does not maintain an explicit gradient-tracking variable; instead, it combines a Nesterov-
type primal–dual extrapolation with multi-round accelerated gossip and ties the batch
size to the gossip depth. Our analysis focuses on making the communication complexity
in this specific design explicit, rather than deriving a general-purpose optimality result
for all gradient-tracking variants.

Multi-round and accelerated gossip. The idea of performing multiple gossip rounds
per iteration to accelerate consensus dates back to early work on multi-consensus meth-
ods and polynomial filtering of the mixing matrix [2, 9]. Chebyshev-accelerated gossip
schemes design a polynomial in the mixing matrix that minimizes the worst-case contrac-
                                                                                  p
tion factor over the spectrum, yielding a number of rounds that scales as O( e 1/ 1 − β)
with the inverse square root of the spectral gap. Such primitives have been used both
as stand-alone averaging mechanisms [2] and as building blocks within decentralized
optimization algorithms [4, 12]. The FGA operator used in the proposed MC-ADSGD
method is an instance of this general idea: it implements an accelerated gossip averaging
procedure with a parameter R controlling the number of internal communication rounds
and a contraction factor ρ that depends explicitly on R and the spectral gap. Our con-
tribution is to embed this primitive into a stochastic accelerated optimization algorithm
and to carry out a Lyapunov analysis that tracks both the disagreement recursion and the
optimization recursion.

Accelerated decentralized methods and communication complexity. Beyond multi-
consensus, several works have investigated acceleration in decentralized optimization,
either via Nesterov-type momentum, dual acceleration, or primal–dual mirror maps [3,
4, 8, 12]. These methods often achieve communication complexity bounds that are opti-
malp(or nearly
            p optimal) in the deterministic strongly convex setting, with a characteris-
tic L/µ/ 1 − β dependence on the condition number and spectral gap. Extensions to
time-varying or directed graphs further refine these guarantees under more general com-
munication models [1, 6]. However, many of these results either assume exact oracles,
lack fully explicit constants in the stochastic regime, or leave the relationship between
the number of communication rounds per iteration and the stochastic batch size implicit.

Positioning of this work. The algorithm proposed in this paper, Multi-Gossip Accel-
erated DSGD (MC-ADSGD), combines several ingredients that have each appeared, in
different forms, in the decentralized optimization literature: local stochastic gradients,
multi-round accelerated gossip, and an accelerated extrapolation structure. Our em-
phasis here is not on claiming a universal improvement over all existing decentralized

methods, but on introducing this particular algorithmic design in the strongly convex
stochastic regime and making the resulting communication-complexity dependence ex-
plicit. In particular, the analysis highlights how the gossip depth R, the spectral gap 1 − β,
and the stochastic noise level jointly enter the final bound, while the heterogeneity term
contributes an explicit residual component.

6     Conclusion
We presented Multi-Gossip Accelerated DSGD, a decentralized stochastic optimization method
that combines accelerated optimization dynamics with accelerated multi-round gossip
communication. The main theorem gives an explicit strongly-convex convergence guar-
antee with transparent dependence on the stepsize, gossip depth, spectral gap, and stochas-
tic variance. Under the homogeneous-optimum condition G ∗ = 0, the resulting communi-
                                                             2
cation complexity
          p      pseparates into a stochastic term of order σ /(µnϵ) and a network term
of order L/µ/ 1 − β, up to logarithmic factors.
    From a methodological viewpoint, the analysis shows that optimization acceleration
and communication acceleration should be designed together: the benefit of the extrap-
olation dynamics appears only when the disagreement recursion is controlled strongly
enough by multi-gossip communication. This is the role of the FGA primitive and the
explicit condition on                 √
                                  ρ = 2(1 − 1 − β ) R .

   A natural next step is to extend the current analysis beyond the strongly convex
regime and to establish analogous explicit rates under weaker convexity assumptions.
Another important direction is to understand whether a similarly explicit accelerated
analysis can be carried out under more general network models, including time-varying
communication patterns.
