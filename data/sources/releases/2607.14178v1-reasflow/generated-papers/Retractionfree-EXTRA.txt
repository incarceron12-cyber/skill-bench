A Retraction-Free EXTRA Method for Decentralized
Optimization on the Stiefel Manifold
ReasFlow Paper Copilot


          Abstract. Decentralized optimization provides a fundamental framework for large-
          scale learning and signal processing with distributed data. We study decentralized
          optimization with orthogonality constraints on the Stiefel manifold and propose RF-
          EXTRA, a distributed retraction-free primal-dual method on static undirected net-
          works. The method combines an approximate gradient mapping for orthogonality-
          constrained optimization with an EXTRA-based decentralized recursion, thereby
          avoiding explicit retractions while preserving a simple communication pattern. On
          the theoretical side, the analysis considers both the averaged iterate and the joint er-
          ror (Xk − Xk ,sk − sk ), and establishes a contractive recursion for the joint error under
          an equivalent norm. This recursion is then used to compare the different terms in
          the descent lemma and finally derive an exact O(1/K ) convergence rate for the aver-
          aged iterate under explicit parameter conditions. Experiments on PCA and low-rank
          matrix completion show that RF-EXTRA compares favorably with the reported decen-
          tralized baselines and exhibits strong communication efficiency on the tested tasks on
          the Stiefel manifold.

1   Introduction
Decentralized optimization [1, 2] studies how a network of agents cooperatively solves a
global problem using local data and local communication. It has become a fundamental
framework in large-scale learning [3], signal processing [4, 5], and control [1], where data
are naturally distributed and centralized aggregation is either costly or undesirable. In
its classical Euclidean form, the goal is to combine computational scalability with com-
munication efficiency while preserving the solution quality of the underlying centralized
model.
    Many decentralized problems are not purely Euclidean. In distributed principal com-
ponent analysis, subspace estimation, and related matrix-learning tasks, the decision

variable is required to have orthonormal columns, and the optimization therefore takes
place on the Stiefel manifold

Such orthogonality constraints also arise in deep models with orthogonal layers [6], nor-
malization mechanisms [7], and low-rank fine-tuning schemes [8]. Accordingly, decen-
tralized optimization on the Stiefel manifold has become an important problem in decen-
tralized learning and matrix optimization.
    A central difficulty is that decentralized primal-dual correction mechanisms and man-
ifold feasibility mechanisms are driven by different principles. Methods in the EXTRA
family are analyzed as primal-dual type recursions, whereas manifold methods typically
rely on tangent projections and retractions to preserve feasibility. Once EXTRA is brought
into a nonconvex problem on the Stiefel manifold, combining these two ingredients is es-
sentially equivalent to analyzing a primal-dual algorithm together with nonconvex man-
ifold constraints and geometry in a single recursion. The convergence analysis is hence
highly nontrivial.

method that preserves the correction mechanism of EXTRA while using a retraction-free
update on the Stiefel manifold. This direction is algorithmically attractive because mod-
ern GPUs are highly optimized for basic linear algebra subprograms (BLAS) [9], whereas
retraction operations such as repeated QR or polar orthogonalization are typically less
GPU-friendly than simple matrix multiplications and additions.

1.1     Related Work
1.1.1   Primal methods and primal-dual methods in decentralized optimization
The development of decentralized optimization began with primal methods such as de-
centralized gradient descent (DGD) [2] and decentralized SGD type variants for stochas-
tic objectives [10, 11], which established the basic consensus optimization framework but
generally suffer from steady state bias under constant stepsizes. To remove that bias, later
primal-dual or correction based methods introduced auxiliary states that couple consen-
sus and optimization more tightly. Representative milestones include gradient tracking
and DGT type methods [12–14], EXTRA [4], exact diffusion [5], NIDS [15], and prox-
PDA [16]. These methods clarified how exactness can be preserved over static networks
while maintaining low per-iteration communication cost.
ReasFlow Paper Copilot / CSIAM Trans. Appl. Math., 1 (2026), pp. 1-27                        3


    The surrounding Euclidean literature further expanded this line through refined anal-
yses for heterogeneous and nonconvex settings [3, 17, 18], compressed communication
[19–23], time varying and directed networks [24, 25], local step variants [26], and graph
design viewpoints [27]. These Euclidean results provide the algorithmic backbone for
our method, since RF-EXTRA keeps EXTRA itself while adapting it to optimization on
the Stiefel manifold.

1.1.2   Distributed optimization on the Stiefel manifold
For orthogonality-constrained problems, decentralized Riemannian methods form the
natural baseline. DRSGD [28] studies decentralized Riemannian gradient descent on the
Stiefel manifold and shows that distributed PCA type tasks can be handled directly in
the manifold setting. More recently, DPRGD [29] and DPRGT [30] study projected de-
centralized Riemannian gradient updates and decentralized proximal gradient tracking
on Riemannian manifolds, respectively; together they provide our main projection based
and tracking based baselines. Riemannian EXTRA (REXTRA) [31] further demonstrates
that EXTRA type primal-dual correction can be extended from Euclidean space to com-
pact submanifolds, but still through retraction or projection based updates.
    Related works further considered quantization and compression [32, 33], stochastic
recursive momentum [34], conjugate gradient and natural gradient directions [35, 36],
local linear consensus behavior on the Stiefel manifold [37], manifold consensus formu-
lations [38,39], and decentralized retraction-free optimization on the Stiefel manifold [40].
However, an EXTRA type correction method in this retraction-free regime is still unavail-
able.

1.1.3   Retraction-free optimization with orthogonality constraints
A separate line of work studies orthogonality-constrained optimization without perform-
ing explicit retractions at every iteration. DESTINY [41] introduced an approximate
gradient mapping for orthogonality-constrained optimization through an approximate
augmented Lagrangian formulation on the Stiefel manifold, where orthogonality is con-
trolled by a penalty-based surrogate instead of being enforced by explicit retractions at
every step. Early centralized retraction-free methods also showed that one can reduce the
cost of repeated QR or polar decompositions while still controlling feasibility drift [42,43].
This perspective is particularly attractive for large matrix problems, where orthogonal-
ization can dominate the local computational budget.
    More recent developments include infeasible methods with local linear convergence
guarantees [44], ambient-space reformulations that dissolve hard constraints into smooth
penalty structures [45], and preconditioned schemes motivated by LoRA type large-
model fine-tuning [8]. Related applications in orthogonal weight normalization and
multi-Stiefel learning further illustrate the practical importance of low-cost updates for
orthogonality-constrained problems [6, 7]. The present work draws on this line to con-
struct the local geometric step, and it combines that step with the decentralized EXTRA
correction mechanism discussed above.

1.2     Contribution and Organization
This paper combines decentralized primal-dual correction, distributed optimization on
the Stiefel manifold, and retraction-free orthogonality control in a single scheme. The
key idea is to embed an approximate gradient mapping for orthogonality-constrained
optimization into an EXTRA recursion.
   Our contributions are summarized as follows.

      • We design and implement RF-EXTRA, a distributed retraction-free EXTRA al-
        gorithm that combines an approximate gradient mapping for orthogonality-
        constrained optimization with an EXTRA-based decentralized recursion on the
        Stiefel manifold, and we validate this design through a systematic numerical study
        on PCA and LRMC over the Stiefel manifold.

      • On the theory side, we show that the joint-error variables (Xk − Xk ,sk − sk ) remain
        in a controllable region and satisfy a contractive recursion under an equivalent
        norm. This allows us to establish a summed joint-error bound, compare the dif-
        ferent terms in the descent analysis of the original objective function, and finally
        derive an exact O(1/K ) convergence rate under a constant stepsize. In contrast
        to existing retraction-based EXTRA-type methods such as REXTRA [31] and exist-
        ing retraction-free schemes without EXTRA correction, our analysis must couple
        retraction-free feasibility control with an EXTRA-type inexact primal-dual recur-
        sion; this combination is nontrivial because of the manifold nonconvexity, espe-
        cially under a constant stepsize, as also noted in [31].

    This article is organized as follows. Section 2 presents RF-EXTRA together with
the approximate gradient mapping for orthogonality-constrained optimization. Sec-
tion 3 first states the standing assumptions and the averaged-sequence ingredients in
Lemma 3.1, then introduces the equivalent-norm lemma and the joint-error recursion,
next proves neighborhood propagation in Lemma 3.4, and finally establishes the exact
O(1/K ) convergence theorem.

We consider the decentralized optimization problem on the Stiefel manifold introduced
in Section 1. Each agent i ∈ {1,...,n} stores a local variable Xi,k ∈ Rd×r and has access only
to its local objective f i . Let W ∈ Rn×n be a symmetric doubly stochastic mixing matrix
associated with a connected undirected graph, and let

                               V = θ In +(1 − θ )W,              θ ∈ (0,1/2].

We use the lifted mixing matrices W and V introduced in the notation section. Our con-
struction starts from the Euclidean correction philosophy of EXTRA and then replaces
the Euclidean gradient with a surrogate compatible with the geometry of the Stiefel man-
ifold.
    For unconstrained consensus optimization, EXTRA corrects the steady state bias of
decentralized gradient descent by coupling the primal recursion with a memory term.
An equivalent two-state representation of the Euclidean mechanism can be written as

space computation while still maintaining effective control of feasibility and consensus
through the penalty term and the correction state.

3     Convergence Analysis
This section establishes convergence of the averaged RF-EXTRA iterate. The analysis
combines two ingredients: descent properties inherited from the centralized surrogate
analysis on the Stiefel manifold and a contractive recursion for the joint error. The latter
is handled in an S-norm, which is essential for the EXTRA subsystem.

5    Conclusion
We proposed RF-EXTRA, a decentralized retraction-free method for optimization on
the Stiefel manifold. The method combines an ambient-space surrogate for the
orthogonality-constrained local models with an EXTRA-based primal-dual recursion,
thereby preserving a simple decentralized communication structure while avoiding ex-
plicit per-iteration retractions.
    On the theoretical side, the analysis is built on the averaged iterate and the joint error
(Xk − Xk ,sk − sk ). By establishing a contractive recursion for the joint error under an equiv-
alent norm and then comparing the different terms in the descent analysis, we obtain an
exact O(1/K ) convergence guarantee for the averaged iterate.
    The experiments indicate that RF-EXTRA is a competitive retraction-free decentral-
ized solver for optimization problems on the Stiefel manifold. On PCA and low-rank
matrix completion, it delivers favorable empirical performance and communication ef-
ficiency relative to the reported baselines. These results support retraction-free decen-

tralized correction as a promising direction for large-scale optimization on matrix mani-
folds.
