## Why this is distinct

The reusable chain is `configured system and trace interface → task/sample/split identity → raw event normalization → induced action vocabulary and compression objective → procedural sequence/program → similarity, fingerprint, or reward query → held-out discrimination or paired intervention → endpoint/artifact/state consequence → stability and transport claim`. A classifier can identify an agent from recurring habits while learning irrelevant harness, verbosity, tool-schema, release-period, or task-family cues. Conversely, two agents can share a compressed procedural signature while differing in evidence quality, judgment, safety, or artifact consequences. A programmed procedural reward is also an intervention on behavior, not independent evidence that an expert procedure was transferred or that changed traces improved the work product.

A full audit should separate raw trace availability, normalization loss, vocabulary induction and seed/corpus dependence, task/instance/family leakage, configured-system and interface cues, within-agent stochastic stability, between-agent discrimination, procedure–outcome association, paired behavior intervention, endpoint preservation, cost, and cross-domain/harness transport. It should reconstruct at least one released paper-time fingerprint or controlled comparison if artifacts permit, then separately inventory post-paper additions without back-projecting them into v1. Existing configured-system, trace, skill-intervention, root/surface, artifact-view, metric, task-health, and validity machinery should host reusable lessons unless an exercised nonduplicate gap is proven.
