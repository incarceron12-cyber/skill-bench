## Substantive finding (triage only)

**JobBench: Aligning Agent Work With Human Will**

- Immutable arXiv record: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26329v1
- Immutable PDF target: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.26329v1
- Official project: https://job-bench.github.io/
- Official evaluation repository: https://github.com/Job-Bench/job-bench-eval
- arXiv and official search metadata describe 130 agentic tasks across 35 occupations, selected around workflows that experts report wanting to delegate. This introduces a demand-side selection construct—delegation desire—that is distinct from GDP-weighted economic importance, observed production demand, expert authorship, or model capability.
- **Evidence status:** metadata/search-result and URL verification only. The PDF and release were not read or inspected during scouting. Counts, recruitment, elicitation method, task construction, environments, graders, baseline results, and worker-benefit claims remain author claims pending full-text and release audit. No claim is made that sampled experts represent occupations, that stated delegation preference survives task transformation, or that benchmark success causes empowerment, satisfaction, productivity, or readiness.

## Benchmark implication to test

A full review should separate occupational frame, participant sampling, elicited delegation preference, selected workflow, task projection, executable environment, grader evidence, score aggregation, and any worker-outcome claim. It should test whether “wanting to delegate” is measured as a stable preference, contextual judgment, or authoring input; whether preference implies consent or authority; and whether task success supports feasibility without silently becoming evidence of worker benefit.
