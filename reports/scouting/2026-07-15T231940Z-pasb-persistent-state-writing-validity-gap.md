## Why this is distinct

The reusable chain is `user/source claim and authority → conversational framing and repetition → assistant acceptance/rejection → write attempt → framework-specific flush → realized durable state delta → retained attribution/status/scope → session reset and leakage check → later retrieval/presentation → adoption/rejection → response or action → state/artifact consequence → affected-party outcome`. MemSyco starts from authored memory supplied to the solver; MemOps scores expected operations and downstream reports. PASB appears to insert the missing actual-write and cross-session-reuse observations. That distinction matters for every expertise-bearing agent: a caveated expert statement, client preference, provisional hypothesis, or workflow may be harmless in one conversation yet become over-authoritative after lossy storage.

A full audit should separate conversational agreement, intended and attempted write, realized bytes/state, flush and reset validity, retrieval and model-visible presentation, semantic adoption, endpoint effect, and consequence. It should test whether “commit” is defined independently of later failure; whether state capture covers every Hermes-Agent/OpenClaw surface; whether framework/model/configuration differences are controlled; whether attribution/status/scope transformations are deterministically derived or judge-inferred; whether human-gold calibration covers state and cross-turn claims; how retries, crashes, parse failures, and uncommitted episodes enter denominators; and whether the cross-domain condition holds content, exposure, and observer policy fixed. Existing authority, memory lifecycle, information-flow, configured-system, trace, state-delta, task-health, metric, and validity machinery should host reusable lessons unless an exercised nonduplicate contract gap is proven.
