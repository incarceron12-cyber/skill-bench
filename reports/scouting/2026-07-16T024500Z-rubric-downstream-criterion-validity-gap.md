## Why this is distinct

The reusable chain is `intended use and stakeholder decision → authorized work-quality construct → criterion/rubric dimensions and weights → judge evidence view and item decisions → independently observed outcome definition, timing, and authority → association and incremental prediction → prospective decision policy → intervention/use → realized utility, burden, and stakeholder consequence`. Reliability or agreement at the judge layer cannot validate the criterion layer. A dimension may correlate with a conversion label while the composite obscures it; conversely, selecting or reweighting dimensions on the same labeled sample can improve in-sample association without establishing prospective transport or decision value. A verified conversion event is stronger than a model preference label but is not automatically customer welfare, causal benefit, productivity, or economic value.

A full audit should reconstruct the Phase 1 and Phase 2 sampling frames and attrition; conversation/customer/agent clustering; conversion definition, attribution window, verification, missingness, and censoring; judge prompt/model/call topology/evidence view; rubric authority and dimension dependence; annotation reliability; multiple testing; effect uncertainty; agent-type, length, and other confounds; in-sample weight search and hard-cap derivation; score direction reversal; incremental value over simple observable baselines; the two operational cycles and whether outcomes were prospectively measured; privacy/consent and selective reporting; and platform/cultural transport. Existing validity, metric, rater, configured-system, longitudinal, task-health, participation, and consequence machinery should host the lessons unless an exercised nonduplicate contract gap is proven.
