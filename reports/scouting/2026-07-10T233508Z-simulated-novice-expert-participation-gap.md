## Benchmark implication to test

A simulated novice may elicit conditional explanations, misconceptions, thresholds, repair strategies, and rationale that a static questionnaire misses. It may also anchor experts to synthetic misconceptions, reward fluent coaching rather than authentic professional judgment, impose hidden moderation labor, or select unusually motivated contributors. A full audit can test whether the paper supplies measured recruitment, burden, engagement, privacy, and knowledge-yield evidence—or only design recommendations—and whether any mechanism transfers to consented benchmark-authoring sessions.
