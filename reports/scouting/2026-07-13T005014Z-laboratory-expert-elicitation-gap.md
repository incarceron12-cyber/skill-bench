## Benchmark implication to test

The source may offer an unusually direct bridge from expert elicitation to executable benchmark primitives: experts' failure conditions and branching logic could become provenance-linked scenarios, conditional checks, source-pack relations, and counterfactual probes rather than prose-only guidance. The key validity question is whether graph nodes and edges preserve expert authority, disagreement, context, exceptions, valid time, and workflow consequences—or merely formalize interviewer/model interpretations. A full review should separate elicitation evidence from representation, queryability from operational usefulness, deployment from measured benefit, and one laboratory setting from cross-domain transfer.
