## Why this is a narrow, useful gap

The reusable evidence chain is:

`recorded procedure/video → transcript and visible action evidence → ontology/rule authority → candidate relation or missing proposition → model/logic transformation → graph insertion and provenance → constraint conformance → independent semantic validation → expert correction/approval → recipient retrieval and adoption → procedural consequence → cross-context transfer`.

This advances charter objectives A, B, D, and E. The repository already insists that observed action, inferred rationale, expert authority, representation correctness, and transfer are distinct, but lacks a deep release-audited source that explicitly claims to turn procedural video into tacit, ontology-grounded knowledge. The source can test whether neuro-symbolic constraints improve source-faithful codification or merely make model-generated graph completion more internally coherent.

The claim ceiling is central. Instructional videos are authored demonstrations, not direct access to tacit judgment; a transcript can omit embodied cues and exception logic; ontology/SHACL compliance is not semantic truth; reference-relative completeness depends on reference authority and closure; active gap filling may increase unsupported propositions; and graph quality does not establish expert approval, recipient usability, execution benefit, professional correctness, or transfer. Manufacturing repair is a bounded proxy for a general transformation-validity question, not a benchmark-domain commitment.
