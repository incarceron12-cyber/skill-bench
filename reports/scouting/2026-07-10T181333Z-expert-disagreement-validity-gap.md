## Why this is distinct

The repository already requires plural immutable labels, observer evidence views, disagreement types, and explicit adjudication lineage. Those controls mainly make resolution inspectable. This source raises a prior validity question: whether resolution is legitimate at all when experts apply defensible but incompatible theories, professional standards, or loss functions. Consensus, majority vote, and averaging can erase construct pluralism rather than remove annotation error.

The reusable question is not whether skill-bench should become a mental-health benchmark. It is how benchmark authoring and grading should distinguish correctable rater error from stable, framework-conditioned professional disagreement across domains; preserve dissent; prevent synthetic aggregate targets; and bound claims when no single expert-endorsed ground truth exists.
