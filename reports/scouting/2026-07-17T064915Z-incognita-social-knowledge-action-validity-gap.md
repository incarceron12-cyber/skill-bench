## Why this is a narrow, useful gap

The corpus already covers synthetic organizational knowledge routing, real-dialogue elicitation, role/permission collaboration, and grounded state benchmarks. Incognita appears to join these into a different evaluation chain:

`role-partitioned knowledge and operation authority → contact opportunity and source selection → question/message → received evidence → belief or plan update → specialist proposal/admission → deterministic state effect → final-state reward and premature-completion diagnosis`.

That chain could distinguish finding the right source, acquiring sufficient evidence, respecting operation authority, acting on acquired information, and stopping appropriately. But stronger contact or elicitation counts do not establish semantic uptake, justified action, correct authority, or task success; inherited tau-bench rewards may omit the social process and the benchmark transformation may alter task information, tools, or difficulty. Simulator roles and deterministic mediation also do not establish real organizational collaboration or tacit expertise. Retail is a bounded substrate for a cross-domain knowledge-to-action validity question, not a benchmark scope commitment.
