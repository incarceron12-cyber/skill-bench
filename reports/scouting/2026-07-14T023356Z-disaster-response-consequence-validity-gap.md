## Benchmark implication to test

High-consequence workflow benchmarks need a consequence-bearing task graph rather than a gold-trajectory similarity shortcut: `event/data provenance and valid time → expert role/authority → public operational mandate → evidence fusion and uncertainty → tool preconditions/arguments/results → accepted alternative plans → protected constraints and affected populations → report/state artifact → independent checks → simulated or observed consequence → licensed claim`. Full review should test whether task and event clusters are kept distinct; whether source freshness, sensor mismatch, road/facility state, uncertainty, and contradictions are represented; whether expert verification covers alternative valid paths and abstention/escalation; whether scoring observes only final fields or also unsafe intermediate actions and unsupported report claims; and whether real-event input improves ecological resemblance without supplying operational or causal validity. Any transfer should reuse existing expertise-transfer, authority, evidence-state, procedural-relation, artifact/evidence-view, action-safety, trace, root/surface, metric, task-health, and validity machinery rather than create a disaster-specific subsystem.
