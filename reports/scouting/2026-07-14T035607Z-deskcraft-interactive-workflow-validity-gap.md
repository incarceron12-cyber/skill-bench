## Benchmark implication to test

Interactive workflow evaluation needs a participant-and-revision chain rather than an undifferentiated “human-in-the-loop” condition: `task/workflow provenance → initial public mandate → uncertainty and information availability → participant identity/authority → interaction opportunity → agent/user initiation → proposition delivered → semantic uptake → attempted action or revision → artifact/state delta and collateral preservation → endpoint quality → participant burden and downstream consequence → licensed claim`. Availability, exercise, usefulness, and burden are different denominators. Full review should test matched no-interaction/interaction conditions, whether clarifications reveal evaluator targets, whether interruptions and feedback are ecologically authorized or simulator-authored treatments, whether post-turn repair preserves unrelated work, and whether artifact/state observers cover professional consequences. Any transfer should reuse existing participation, authority, information-flow, artifact/state, transition, trace, metric, task-health, root/surface, and validity machinery rather than create a DeskCraft-specific subsystem.
