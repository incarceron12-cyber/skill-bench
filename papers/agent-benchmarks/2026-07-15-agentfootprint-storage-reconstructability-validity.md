## One-sentence contribution

AgentFootprint correctly separates **logical volume generated by a configured agent trajectory** from **persistence-layer amplification**, and measures retained bytes jointly with task success and conversation-history reconstruction; its strongest evidence makes storage a necessary resource dimension, not a scalar efficiency ranking, because its `R` score preserves only one narrow utility while setup, remote retention, full recovery, audit semantics, write amplification, deletion, and long-lived operational state remain outside or only partially observed.

## Why this matters for skill-bench

Real knowledge-work trials leave more than the requested deliverable: tool results, source copies, checkpoints, logs, intermediate artifacts, memories, debug traces, caches, and evaluator evidence. These retained states affect:

- whether a trial can be audited, resumed, handed off, or diagnosed;
- whether private or licensed evidence can be deleted at the required scope;
- whether a desktop or fleet can operate within storage limits;
- whether repeated trials contaminate one another;
- whether benchmark operation can preserve enough evidence without retaining every raw representation.

The transferable object is not “disk bytes” alone. It is a **retention-utility record**:

```text
configured trial + declared filesystem/remote boundary
→ attributable workspace artifacts and framework/evaluator residue
→ logical objects and representation copies
→ persistence and growth policy
→ supported reconstruction/recovery/audit/deletion operations
→ retained-byte/time/cost burden
→ bounded operating claim
```

This review advances charter objectives A and C through targeted expansion into an unmeasured operating resource and executable release validation. Software-agent frameworks are the methodological case; retention identity and evidence utility transfer across spreadsheet, document, browser, research, and enterprise workflows and do not narrow `skill-bench` to coding.

## Research question and defensible claim boundary

The paper asks how much post-run local data agent frameworks retain, why raw byte measurement misses semantic duplication, how retention grows with horizon, what retained bytes support, and whether the persistence layer can be separated from agent behavior (Sections 1, 3–7, pp. 1–9).

It contributes:

1. a per-run boundary over fresh workspace and redirected home/XDG state;
2. six reported measurements: total retention `S_total`, channel composition, duplication factor `D`, growth exponent `α`, compressibility `C`, and conversation-history reconstructability `R`;
3. eight framework/version configurations under shared tasks, tools, model backend, and nominal temperature;
4. 1,061 reported sandboxes across main, ablation, task-family, backend, growth, fixed-trace, shared-thread, and reconstruction suites;
5. a public-trajectory-volume analysis over 108 normalized SWE-bench Verified submissions;
6. a content-addressed-store (CAS) existence proof and a de-identified production case.

The strongest defensible empirical claim is:

> Under the authors' versioned quickstart-style adapters, local-retention boundary, synthetic tasks, model/provider conditions, and reported run-selection policy, documented persistence configurations that often had equal task success retained materially different local bytes; serialization-aware calibration showed that raw-file deduplication can miss exact logical duplication; fixed-content and repeated-horizon controls associated persistence policy with amplification and growth; and the released subset supports offline recomputation of the main file-QA table, heterogeneous strict-joint cells, and synthetic meter calibration.

The evidence does **not** establish framework quality; general reasoning capability; capability-normalized storage efficiency; population prevalence of storage behavior; production cost optimality; complete audit, replay, recovery, or deletion utility; causal framework effects independent of adapters/configuration; general scaling laws; equivalence of exports and runtime residue; or a universal storage–success relationship.

## Methodology and system

### Boundary, attribution, and configured-system identity

Each `(framework, task, repetition)` uses a fresh workspace and redirected `HOME`/XDG directories. Setup runs first, the harness snapshots file sizes and—in released meter v3—SHA-256 values, the adapter executes, and the meter includes new or changed workspace/home files while excluding named package/model/cache patterns (`runner.py` lines 83–152; `meter.py` lines 243–355; Sections 3.1 and 4.3, pp. 3–5).

This is a meaningful attribution boundary, not a full sandbox. System temporary paths and remote telemetry are outside it; hosted OpenAI tracing is explicitly disabled. Setup-time staging is baselined away. InfiAgent copies about 0.60 MB of task corpus into managed state before the snapshot, so its reported file-QA mean is 2.12 MB under the execution-only boundary and 2.72 MB under the disclosed total-operational view (Appendix N, pp. 14–15). Apparent file length is measured, not allocated blocks, bytes written, peak disk, copy-on-write amplification, or byte-days.

The roster pins package versions and compares documented durable-session recipes rather than storage-tuned configurations. That supports a **default configured-experience** comparison. It does not isolate framework abstractions: prompts, adapter APIs, model-call topology, tool budgets, persistence channels, restore APIs, and what each vendor calls “durable” differ. The paper acknowledges that equal `R` does not equal equal capability (Sections 4.2 and 8, pp. 4–5, 9; Appendix R, p. 16).

### Tasks, runs, and outcomes

The principal file-QA suite supplies ten synthetic 600 KB corpora and five sequential exact-code questions per task, using an instructed `A,B,A,C,A` reread pattern. Additional suites include constant-file repeated observations at `T={25,50,100,200}`, five minimal write tasks, exploratory retrieval, and five-task edit/data families with joint answer-plus-workspace-artifact checks. Immediate success is exact substring or spec-exact deterministic grading (Section 4.1, p. 4; Appendix D, pp. 11–12).

The paper accounts for 509 main-study runs and 552 replication/variant runs. File-QA has three repetitions per framework–task cell. The full-history growth trio has three seeds per horizon; most other growth frameworks and heterogeneous cells have one run. Invalid provider incidents are screened and rerun, with rejected attempts reportedly retained; ten congested SmolAgents runs also use an extended wall-clock limit. One Qwen2.5-7B SmolAgents cell switches from the shared provider to self-hosted vLLM because tool schemas were unsupported (Appendices C, F, and G, pp. 10–12). These are disclosed but make service policy part of the configured comparison.

### Serialization-aware storage metrics

`S_total` is retained apparent bytes, split into workspace and home-side residue. Channel composition uses adapter-audited path rules. The decisions/observations/derivations/duplicates taxonomy is interpretive; the implementation does not semantically classify each byte (Section 3.1, p. 3).

For `D`, the meter extracts SQLite text/blob cells longer than 64 bytes, JSONL lines, or whole other files; applies FastCDC with SHA-256; and computes logical bytes divided by unique chunk bytes (`meter.py` lines 105–173 and 305–348). This appropriately avoids SQLite page and JSON-escaping artifacts, but it is an exact-content lower bound:

- compressed, encrypted, delta-encoded, schema-transformed, reordered, and near-duplicate representations can evade it;
- SQLite headers, indexes, free pages, short cells, numeric cells, and `NULL`s do not enter logical `D`, although physical overhead remains in `S_total`;
- a cross-representation probe/echo count supplements `D`, but double/triple escaping remains a lower bound.

`C` concatenates retained files through zstd level 19 with a long window. It is a compressibility bound, not a directly usable archive. `α` is a log–log fit over four horizons; the paper correctly limits it to superlinear behavior over the measured range rather than a scaling law (Sections 3.2 and 5.2, pp. 3, 6; Appendix G, pp. 12–13).

### Reconstructability and operational recovery

Automated `R` is coarse:

- `R=0`: no attributed retained files;
- `R=1`: retained bytes but no recognized per-call structure;
- `R=2`: recognized per-call structure without at least 90% line-probe coverage in one channel;
- `R=3`: recognized per-call structure with a complete observation copy candidate.

The released scorer recognizes framework-specific filename hints and SQLite names, tests sampled corpus lines under raw/single/double escaping, and uses task files to create probes (`replay_probe.py` lines 31–148). Its own synthetic validation gives `R=3` to shuffled-order and missing-tool-argument stores by design. Therefore paper-level `R=3` additionally depends on framework-specific extractors and an external HTTP proxy archive: with call boundaries supplied by that proxy, the authors compare reconstructed roles, contents, tool-call IDs/arguments/results, and order over 194 requests. Six configurations pass. A separate between-turn nonce probe shows those six can resume in a fresh process; CrewAI cannot (Section 5.4, pp. 7–8; Appendices I, J, and O, pp. 13–15).

This is stronger than counting logs, but “retained bytes alone reconstruct exact step-k input” is too broad. Call boundaries come from external proxy evidence; some system prompts remain in code configuration; the contract reconstructs ordered conversation content, not the complete provider request; and it does not test mid-turn tool/workflow recovery, environment state, executable replay, provenance completeness, redaction, access control, audit query utility, or deletion.

### Fixed trace and content-addressed mitigation

The fixed-trace mock endpoint holds three reads, 182.7 KB of observations, and one answer constant across seven persisting adapters. Reported retained bytes per observation span 1.03× to 6.86×; sequential rather than batched LangGraph transport raises amplification to 7.40× (Section 5.6, p. 8; Appendix L, p. 14). This is the cleanest persistence-layer control, but framework adapters still translate schemas and termination conventions, and transport shape is itself a treatment. It isolates the measured adapter/configuration path, not a framework essence.

The approximately 300-line CAS postprocessor externalizes JSON/JSONL/log strings and SQLite text/blob cells of at least 1 KB by SHA-256, compresses objects, restores them, and checks JSON object equality, selected SQLite user-table row-set equality, or pass-through bytes (`cas_compactor.py`; Section 7, pp. 8–9). Seventy released reports claim 4.73–36.34× per-run reductions with zero local restore failures; paper aggregation reports 4.8–32.7× means. Coarse `R` grades are preserved after restoration.

This is a useful existence proof, not demonstrated framework-ready storage. Frameworks cannot consume CAS references in place; SQLite FTS/internal tables are treated as regenerable; row-set equality does not preserve row order or prove every index, trigger, workflow state, event relation, deletion behavior, authorization boundary, or audit operation. Unsalted content hashes create equality, tenant, and erasure risks that the paper appropriately notes (Appendix A, p. 10).

## Evidence and reported results

### Controlled paper results

On 30 file-QA runs per configuration, documented persistent defaults range from CrewAI's 0.32 MB to LangGraph's 5.10 MB among five 100%-accurate persisting configurations—a reported 15.7× ratio with bootstrap interval `[15.0,16.3]`. SmolAgents retains zero and scores `R=0`; CrewAI scores `R=1`; six systems score `R=3`. `D` ranges from 1.5 to 12.8 among persistent defaults. Disabling persistence on a five-task subset preserves immediate accuracy for four systems and keeps AutoGen within observed variance while driving bytes and `R` to zero (Sections 5.1 and 5.3, pp. 5–7).

At 200 repeated observations, the three full-history configurations retain approximately 56–323 MB and fit `α=1.74–1.95`; windowed or append-once configurations fit near-linear/sublinear values over this workload. A continuous ten-task shared thread leaves 95.2 MB for LangGraph versus 5.8 MB across ten fresh fixed-trace sandboxes, showing that fresh-run attribution can understate long-lived checkpoint growth (Appendices G and M, pp. 12–14).

On edit/data tasks, five configurations achieve full joint success and still span reported 6.9× and 14.1× retention. This matters because it distinguishes workspace deliverable presence from home-side framework state: InfiAgent answers correctly but puts artifacts outside the required shared workspace (Section 5.5, p. 8; Appendix D, pp. 11–12).

### Wild and production evidence

The SWE-bench analysis normalizes 108 of 134 snapshot submissions and reports exported bytes per instance spanning 1,617× with Kendall `τ_b=0.080`, permutation `p=0.222`. This establishes export-policy heterogeneity in that snapshot. It does not establish on-disk residue or that storage cannot improve success: systems differ in tasks covered, export schemas, configurations, families, and other capability-relevant dimensions; absence of detectable monotonic association is not equivalence or causal null evidence (Section 6, p. 8).

The production appendix reports a de-identified eight-task 442 MB InfiAgent root, a 131 MB quoting task with 79.2 MB residue and 2.6 MB delivered artifacts, and an operator-attested approximately 56 GB over approximately 1,000 rounds. This is valuable existence evidence, but one deployment, selected measurement root, uncalibrated “round,” whitelist collector, de-identified aggregates, and no comparator or outcome utility cannot estimate production prevalence, total lifecycle cost, or optimal retention (Appendix P, pp. 15–16).

### What the pinned release independently supports

A fresh virtual environment produced:

- `verify_expected.py`: **PASS** for the eight-framework/240-run main table, 16 heterogeneous strict-joint cells, and exact-scene calibration;
- synthetic `D=5` stores: serialization-aware `D=5.0` while naïve raw-file `D` is `1.62–2.40` for SQLite/single/double/triple escaping;
- small repeated lines: measured `D=8.0` while naïve `D=1.02`;
- near duplicates: exact-match `D=1.0` while `C=17.672`, confirming metric complementarity;
- automated `R` fixtures: intended coarse grades pass, while shuffled order and missing arguments remain intentionally invisible;
- available seed-1 growth: `α=1.72` LangGraph, `1.76` AutoGen, `1.95` LlamaIndex.

The release contains 301 `pilot_runs` measurements, 37 long-horizon measurements, 40 each for natural/edit/data/write suites, 70 CAS reports, seven synthetic `R` cases, and six reconstructed-history JSON files. This is unusually inspectable and enough to audit substantial machinery.

### Release-to-paper gaps

The public snapshot is not the complete revision-matched supplement described by Appendix A and `SUPPLEMENTARY_README.md`:

1. `MANIFEST.txt`, explicitly cited for version alignment, is absent.
2. Claimed `representative_stores/` raw stores are absent.
3. Only seed 1 is present for the three repeated growth configurations; paper seeds 2/3 are absent.
4. `fixed_trace_runs/summary.json` contains only OpenAI Agents, while the paper reports seven batched configurations; the sequential summary contains only LangGraph.

6. The benchmark card says legacy delta accounting was audited over 501 runs, while the paper and Appendix N say 509 main-study sandboxes.

Thus the public release partially recomputes reported results but does not independently reproduce all 1,061 reported sandboxes, repeated uncertainty, fixed-trace table, or complete reconstruction validation.

## Unique insight: optimize a typed retention frontier, not bytes or `R`

AgentFootprint's deepest transferable insight is its decomposition:

```text
retained bytes = logical trajectory/evidence volume × persistence amplification
```

The fixed trace identifies the second term more cleanly than end-to-end task comparisons. But the paper's proposed “exact reconstruction at minimal bytes” target is still incomplete. Retention supports multiple, non-substitutable operations:

```text
history reconstruction
≠ model-request reconstruction
≠ executable replay
≠ between-turn resume
≠ mid-turn/workflow recovery
≠ artifact provenance
≠ causal diagnosis
≠ compliance audit
≠ selective deletion
≠ handoff usability
```

A smaller store dominates a larger one only when both preserve the **same authorized information and operational utility** under matched access latency, restore cost, failure recovery, audit queries, security boundaries, deletion semantics, and retention horizon. `R=3` matches one history-content predicate; it does not capability-normalize the store.

For `skill-bench`, storage therefore belongs in a plural configured-system resource frontier, alongside task quality, reliability, latency, inference/human cost, safety, and evidence utility. A useful benchmark should expose avoidable representation amplification without incentivizing deletion of evidence needed to validate the score itself.

## Limitations and validity threats

### Boundary and attribution

- Setup-time corpus duplication is excluded from headline bytes, remote telemetry is disabled or out of scope, and system temp paths are not captured.
- Fresh homes improve attribution but differ from long-lived production stores; the shared-thread result demonstrates potentially much larger cumulative behavior.
- `S_total` is post-run stock, not peak storage, bytes written, write amplification, I/O latency, backup replication, remote retention, allocated blocks, retention duration, or deletion burden.
- Workspace output is labeled an artifact by location, not by professional legitimacy, authority, provenance, or downstream use.

### Construct and metric validity

- Exact CDC deduplication misses semantic, near-duplicate, compressed, encrypted, delta, and transformed copies; probe echo is incomplete under nested encoding.
- `D` excludes physical SQLite structures and short/numeric cells. It must be read with `S_total`, not as a physical amplification ratio.
- `C` is order-dependent concatenated compressibility and not a usable random-access representation.
- Four horizons cannot identify a functional form; `α` is workload-, archive-policy-, and transport-conditioned.
- Automated `R` depends on names/path hints and task-derived probes and cannot check order or arguments. Validated paper grades rely on additional adapter-specific extractors and proxy evidence.
- The nonce probe tests between-turn conversational continuation, not crash consistency during writes, tool state, workflow graph position, idempotence, or output recovery.

### Comparison and inference

- “Documented durable default” is a curated protocol choice, not a unique natural treatment. AutoGen's event logger, save cadence, adapter transport, and InfiAgent's multi-channel stores illustrate reasonable alternatives.
- Framework effects are bundled with adapter implementation, versions, call topology, prompts, budgets, model/service behavior, and persistence capability bundles.
- File-QA intentionally provokes rereads and has a simple exact endpoint; exploratory and heterogeneous suites broaden only modestly and mostly have one attempt per task.
- The headline bootstrap does not create a framework population or capability-normalized estimand; repeated runs and tasks are dependent within configured systems.
- Provider incidents are selectively rerun under guards or longer limits; the preservation of rejected attempts is good, but acceptance/rerun policy changes trial realization.
- Backend replication preserves rank descriptively but one cell changes serving infrastructure and weak-model overflows couple context policy, success, and storage.

### CAS and utility preservation

- CAS restoration verifies selected representation equality, not in-place framework operation, all SQLite semantics, workflow/event provenance, query latency, concurrency, crash safety, deletion, authorization, or tenant separation.
- Coarse `R` preservation after restoration does not prove preservation of every operational capability supported by the original default.
- Hash-addressed deduplication can leak content equality and complicate deletion; cross-tenant use is particularly unsafe without stronger design.
- The release's per-run CAS reductions are an existence proof over retained snapshots, not a production storage system or total-cost evaluation.

### Reproducibility and operational realism

- Positive: immutable full paper, pinned code, package locks, deterministic generators, rich per-run measurement records, synthetic calibration, offline table verifier, invalid-attempt discussion, and explicit claim limitations.
- Negative: absent manifest/raw representative stores, incomplete growth/fixed-trace records, unavailable complete proxy ground truth, and no one-command reconstruction of all paper figures/tables from the public snapshot.
- The main runs use one Apple workstation and one provider route. Local bytes are hardware-insensitive in principle, but framework writes, SQLite behavior, package paths, service errors, and cache policies can vary by OS/filesystem/runtime.
- Production evidence is one operator-authorized case with aggregate attestations and no independent comparator, outcome measure, or deletion exercise.

## Relation to existing evidence

- **Configured-system identity:** AgentFootprint reinforces that model, framework, adapter, tool topology, persistence channels, archival policy, environment boundary, and feedback policy need separate hashes. “Framework” is too coarse.
- **Harness-Bench:** redirected `HOME` and fresh workspaces improve attribution but are not enforced host containment. Storage measurement requires outer-envelope canaries for temp paths, network telemetry, mounted caches, and remote traces.
- **Workspace-Bench and artifact contracts:** delivered artifacts, source-pack copies, hidden framework state, evaluator evidence, and collateral mutations must remain distinct. Location alone does not identify artifact authority or utility.
- **MemoryArena, LongMemEval, and EvoMemBench:** model-facing memory efficacy is different from system-wide persistence. A memory treatment can reduce prompt context while increasing checkpoint/log residue, or retain bytes without supporting later action.
- **STRACE, task health, and validity arguments:** traces are evidence for diagnosis and adjudication. Aggressive storage minimization can destroy the basis for score validity, while redundant raw copies can increase privacy and deletion risk.
- **Reliability and metric monitoring:** storage requires population/window, invalid-run, retry, clustering, quantile, threshold, and action semantics. A mean per clean sandbox does not estimate fleet retention or tail risk.
- **Inference-cost evidence:** token/API cost is a flow; retention is a stock. Both need matched system boundaries and utility-normalized frontiers rather than isolated minimization.

## Transfer to skill-bench

### Retain

1. Capture a before/after manifest over task workspace, agent home/state, evaluator outputs, and declared remote channels for every valid trial.
2. Separate requested deliverables from framework residue, evaluator evidence, caches, staged inputs, and shared environment assets.
3. Record logical-object identity and representation lineage so raw bytes, escaped logs, snapshots, summaries, and CAS references can be related.
4. Use a fixed-trace or recorded-event replay condition to distinguish trajectory volume from persistence amplification.
5. Measure growth over repeated stages and long-lived shared stores, not only fresh isolated tasks.
6. Report storage jointly with success, reliability, recovery/audit utility, latency, inference cost, human review, safety, and validity status.
7. Preserve invalid attempts and rerun policy; storage left by failed/service-invalid trials still creates operational obligations.

### Repair

1. **Declare the full boundary:** local paths, temp paths, remote telemetry, setup/staging, shared caches, backups, evaluator stores, and post-run cleanup.
2. **Type retained objects:** deliverable, source copy, observation, decision, derivation, checkpoint, trace, grader evidence, cache, credential/secrets risk, duplicate, and unknown.
3. **Specify utility predicates:** exact conversation content, full provider request, executable replay, between-/mid-turn resume, workflow recovery, provenance query, diagnosis, handoff, selective deletion, and legal hold.
4. **Measure lifecycle stock:** bytes after run, peak/write volume where feasible, retained byte-days, growth under shared identities, restore latency/cost, and deletion/garbage-collection outcome.
5. **Validate transformations:** require source/target hashes, representation schema, authorized scope, exact and semantic invariants, negative fixtures, alternate encodings, and end-to-end consuming-system tests.
6. **Avoid a scalar rank:** compare systems only under matched task/evidence utility or report a Pareto frontier with explicit non-dominance.
7. **Protect evidence:** define the minimum trace needed for score audit/task-health adjudication, then test whether compaction preserves those exact operations.
8. **Protect people and data:** scope content-addressed stores by tenant/purpose, avoid equality leakage, bind reference-count deletion, and verify that prohibited/private source atoms are not retained in unauthorized representations.

### Test

Use one existing multi-artifact pilot to create a five-condition conformance slice:

- full raw trace/default persistence;
- no persistence;
- exact-content CAS compaction;
- summary-only compaction;
- selective policy retaining decisions and artifact provenance but deleting raw private observations.

Run identical recorded trajectories through each condition. Assert separate outcomes for artifact correctness, exact request reconstruction, executable replay, between-turn resume, root-cause localization, grader replay, handoff reconstruction, private-atom non-retention, selective deletion, restore latency, and retained bytes. Include nested JSON, SQLite, transformed near-duplicates, changed same-size files, remote-telemetry canaries, shared-thread growth, failed trials, and invalid service attempts. Useful completion would reveal which compact representation truly dominates under a declared utility set rather than merely shrinking bytes.

## Bottom line

AgentFootprint identifies a genuine benchmark blind spot. Persistent state is not inference cost, memory-bank size, or deliverable size; it is a compounding configured-system resource that can enable audit and recovery while imposing storage, privacy, governance, and deletion obligations. Its serialization-aware meter, fixed-trajectory control, shared-thread stress test, and joint `S_total`/success/`R` reporting are strong design contributions. Offline calibration confirms that naïve raw-byte deduplication badly misses exact logical repetition.

The paper does not establish a storage-efficiency leaderboard. Defaults bundle different capabilities, `R` covers only a narrow reconstruction predicate, the local boundary excludes setup/remote/temp state, and the public snapshot does not contain every reported run or proxy reconstruction artifact. The CAS experiment preserves selected representations and coarse `R` after restoration, not complete operational or audit equivalence.

For `skill-bench`, retain storage as a first-class plural resource, but optimize it only under typed evidence-utility, privacy, recovery, deletion, and lifecycle constraints. The benchmark must know not just how many bytes remain, but **what information they encode, why it is authorized to persist, which validation or recovery operations they support, and whether those operations still work after compaction or deletion**.
