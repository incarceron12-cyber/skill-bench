## Why this matters for skill-bench

This review advances charter objectives A–C through a bounded context-state validity test: it asks what a plan-removal intervention actually identifies, which state and consequence links it observes, and what reusable instrumentation a knowledge-work benchmark needs. ALFWorld and HotpotQA are method test beds, not a proposal to narrow `skill-bench` to embodied text games or question answering.

## One-sentence contribution

The paper introduces replay pairing, a forced-prefix hidden-state comparison between plan-present and plan-stripped histories; identifies reasoning traces as a path by which removed treatment information can leak back into a control condition; trains linear probes against the paired distance; and tests full, recent-window, plan-protected, and probe-gated context policies in ALFWorld.

**One-sentence assessment:** The paired-history perturbation and contamination diagnosis are useful, but forced replay observes a fading last-token representation difference caused by deleting an entire plan exchange—not proposition-level plan persistence or behavioral use—and the endpoint compression experiment deletes much more than the plan, so the latent, behavioral, and success results do not form the causal chain claimed.

## Research question and defensible claim

The paper asks whether an explicit plan remains represented across later agent turns after the original plan text is removed, whether a hidden-state probe can detect the resulting decay, how reasoning traces contaminate that measurement, and whether context policies that evict or restore the plan change ALFWorld success.

The strongest defensible claim is:

> For selected ReAct trajectories generated by Llama-3.1 models, removing the entire guard-plus-plan exchange from an otherwise forced action/observation history produces a large last-token residual-stream cosine difference at the first subsequent call and a much smaller difference after additional fixed turns. Prior reasoning traces reduce that contrast in reasoning models because they reintroduce plan-related text. Separately, retaining only four recent messages substantially lowers ALFWorld success relative to full context, and neither retaining nor conditionally re-injecting the original plan repairs that broad information loss.

This does **not** establish that a proposition-level plan was encoded and then forgotten, that the same obligations ceased to guide freely chosen actions, that the probe detects plan content rather than phase/position/discourse, that plan eviction caused the endpoint loss, or that the tested monitor is a valid context-management policy.

## Methodology and system

### 1. Plan elicitation and replay pairing

The primary system is Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct running a two-shot ReAct prompt in text-only ALFWorld. At environment step 2, the harness injects a guard asking for a complete ordered plan. For each resulting trajectory, the authors construct:

1. **A, plan-present:** the original generated trajectory with the guard and plan retained in history; and
2. **B, plan-stripped forced replay:** the guard exchange is removed, while A's actions and observations are fed back verbatim. B's generated output is discarded.

At each step and sampled layer, the paper computes `1 − cosine(h_A, h_B)` at the final token. Pre-plan calls are identical and correctly produce near-zero differences. The main 70B collection contains 80 ALFWorld tasks and 1,360 post-plan step records; HotpotQA contributes 30 trajectory pairs and 238 reported step records. Hidden states are captured on 8×H200 GPUs.

This pairing usefully fixes the external action/observation sequence. It prevents behavioral divergence from immediately destroying comparability and makes the conditioning perturbation inspectable. But it also removes the very outcome needed to test plan use: B never controls the next action. Replay pairing is therefore an **observer experiment over forced histories**, not a behavioral counterfactual.

The treatment is also larger than plan content. B differs in:

- missing guard instructions and missing generated plan;
- shorter history and shifted token positions;
- changed discourse structure and turn count;
- potentially changed attention and cache geometry; and
- for reasoning models, a different retained reasoning-history policy.

The paper explicitly acknowledges the position/length/discourse mixture and calls the cosine distance an upper bound on a content-specific component. It reports no length-matched filler, irrelevant-plan, shuffled-plan, paraphrase, corrupted-plan, clause ablation, or matched-position control.

### 2. What “persistence” can mean here

A standard decoder-only transformer invocation does not carry an unobserved recurrent hidden state from one agent call to the next; later calls recompute representations from the retained token history (and an equivalent KV cache, if used). The measured object is therefore not a durable state cell decaying between forward passes. It is the **difference between final-token representations induced by two different histories at successive forced prefixes**.

A shrinking difference can arise because intervening actions and observations dominate the current-token representation, because position/discourse effects attenuate, because later text restates task-relevant content, or because the deleted exchange no longer changes the local prediction. It does not by itself show which plan propositions were represented, whether they were recoverable, or whether they would alter an action distribution.

This distinction matters because the paper sometimes moves from “plan-exchange presence has less effect on the final-token residual” to “the plan is nearly gone,” “has no internal backup,” and “must be read back from the window.” The first statement is directly measured; the latter statements require content and action controls that are absent.

### 3. Hidden-state results

For Llama-3.1-70B on 80 ALFWorld tasks, the manuscript reports mean all-layer signal of `0.453 ± 0.039` at step +1, `0.110 ± 0.032` at +2, and roughly `0.027` by +5. The step+1 layer profile peaks at layer 32 with `0.686 ± 0.024`. Six ALFWorld task-type curves are described as similar. HotpotQA starts at 0.445 and falls to 0.036 at +2; Llama-3.1-8B on 20 ALFWorld tasks reports a similar shape.

These are substantial conditioning differences. However:

- the unit behind the displayed standard errors is not fully specified for every figure;
- trajectory lengths vary, but per-offset survivor counts and termination conditioning are not reported;
- plans are not decomposed into obligations, prerequisites, objects, destinations, constraints, or alternative paths;
- observation–plan overlap is proposed as an explanation for cross-domain decay but is not measured;
- no action-logit, action-ranking, plan-recall, clause entailment, or intervention consequence is linked to the cosine distance; and
- task families, generated-plan quality, plan length, and exact task IDs are unreleased.

The cross-domain difference therefore shows transport of a history perturbation shape, not domain-invariant plan semantics.

### 4. Probe construction, dependence, and leakage

A Ridge probe receives plan-present L32 states and predicts the paired cosine-distance target. The paper reports five-fold stratified cross-validation over 1,360 steps from 80 tasks, `R² = 0.875 ± 0.016`, and a thresholded active-versus-decayed AUROC of 0.999. The ALFWorld direction transfers to HotpotQA with AUROC 1.000, while fixed-threshold precision differs by destination layer and improves after isotonic calibration on 10 HotpotQA tasks, evaluated on 20.

The authors deserve credit for disclosing that step index is almost perfectly decodable (`R² = 0.978` at L32) and that the mixed-effects control was not run. The remaining interpretation is nevertheless weaker than stated:

1. **Target leakage through phase:** plan-signal magnitude is defined by a curve strongly coupled to step offset. Predicting the target can largely mean predicting trajectory phase.
2. **Cluster leakage risk:** the paper says five-fold stratified cross-validation over step records, not task-grouped cross-validation. If steps from one task occur in both train and validation folds, the effective independent sample is closer to 80 trajectories than 1,360 rows.
3. **Split ambiguity:** §3.5 states 50 ALFWorld tasks for training and 30 held out, while §5/Table 1 report cross-validation over all 80. The role of the 30 held-out tasks is not reconciled for the headline probe.
4. **Artificial calibration:** near-perfect Brier/ECE for a wide, step-defined +1 versus ≥+3 contrast does not establish calibration for content loss, later failure, or intervention decisions.
5. **No proposition labels:** a probe trained against the paired distance can only reproduce that instrument; it is not independently supervised to detect which requirement survives.

The lag analysis adds a behavioral-looking layer but remains weak. A single Claude Opus 4-family judge labels first plan-alignment below 0.3; 31/80 trajectories deviate, and the probe reportedly leads by 4.45 steps on average. The judge prompt, annotations, agreement, plan quality, task clustering, and uncertainty are absent. Probe “fire” is also semantically unstable across the paper: binary sections discuss detecting active signal, while the intervention fires when predicted signal falls below a threshold. A threshold selected on the same phase-correlated instrument is not validated merely because it precedes one model judge's deviation label.

### 5. Reasoning-trace confound and strict stripping

The strongest methodological contribution is the observation that forced replay can silently reintroduce the removed treatment. DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-70B restates plan-related material inside prior `<think>` blocks. Standard B replay retains those blocks, so the nominal plan-absent history is not actually plan-evidence absent.

Strict stripping removes complete prior `<think>...</think>` blocks from B only while preserving post-think Thought/Action text. On five in-sample ALFWorld tasks, the manuscript reports step+1 signal increasing from 0.022 to 0.058 (+163%); five held-out tasks show +153%. Non-reasoning Llama changes +4.8%. A Llama-trained probe applied to 19 R1 strict-strip records reaches AUROC 0.748; R1-specific probes over 85 step records reach 1.000. Qwen3-thinking exhibits a different trajectory, with later cosine distance rising rather than spike-decay.

This is useful evidence that **treatment contamination can travel through derived traces**, not only through the original source span. Yet strict stripping is itself asymmetric and broad: it removes all reasoning-history content from B, not only plan propositions. It changes length, discourse, local distribution, error history, and potentially task-state reconstruction. The Llama no-op only shows that a model without `<think>` blocks is unaffected by an operator with nothing to remove; it cannot rule out the asymmetry or generic reasoning-history mechanisms in R1.

The required controls are exactly the ones the paper names but does not run: strip both conditions; replace removed blocks with matched neutral content; redact only proposition-linked plan spans; and compare irrelevant, corrupted, or contradiction-bearing plan restatements. The result supports a contamination warning, not a corrected estimate of latent plan content.

The self-probe denominators are also step records nested in very few tasks (`n=85` records), and the 89.3° direction comparison does not prove a distinct concept. Different architectures, layers, representation bases, and broad treatments can rotate a linear contrast without changing its semantics.

### 6. Behavioral and intervention evidence

A six-condition, 25-task “Phase 1” behavioral sweep is not replay-paired. Reasoning conditions use three stochastic runs at temperature 0.7; non-reasoning baselines use one deterministic run at temperature 0. The reported task successes are 0/25 for both R1 conditions, 0/25 versus 2/25 for Qwen3 thinking raw/strict, and 3/25 versus 2/25 for Llama/Qwen no-thinking baselines. The paper correctly calls effects small and nonsignificant.

These cells do not isolate strict stripping or reasoning mode: trajectories are independent, run counts and decoding differ, model families differ, and the post-processor retains only matched cells with equal step counts. The appendix also discloses that raw-log `success` fires on step-limit truncation, so the analysis redefines success as `max_t score(t) > 0`. Catching this implementation mismatch is valuable, but without released rows it cannot be audited.

Residual-stream steering is a directional null. Across 30 held-out tasks × five paired runs, uniform L32 intervention at α=0.5 changes success by +1.33 points with a wide interval and no significance; larger magnitudes hurt. Head-aware interventions are similarly noisy or destabilize generation. This is evidence against treating the probe direction as a simple causal knob. It also reinforces that linear detectability of a history contrast does not establish plan-content encoding or controllability.

### 7. Compression stress test

The endpoint experiment uses 30 ALFWorld tasks × five runs × four policies under a 20-step cap:

- `none`: full context, 56.7% success;
- `naive`: system prompt plus four recent messages, 22.0%;
- `plan_protected`: recent-four policy plus a pinned plan, 20.7%; and
- `probe_gated`: recent-four policy with conditional plan re-injection, 24.7%.

Task-level paired permutation tests and bootstrap intervals show a clear full-context versus recent-four gap and no recovery from either plan policy. Integrity accounting reports 150 runs per policy with no errored or zero-step runs.

This is useful operational evidence that an aggressive recent-message window destroys information needed for this configured ALFWorld agent. It is **not** an isolated plan-eviction test. Naive compression removes most prior observations, actions, errors, object locations, achieved subgoals, and working state together with the plan. The paper acknowledges this after the plan-aware conditions fail.

Consequently, the 34.7-point gap cannot validate the hidden-state decay mechanism or the statement that “agents keep no usable plan once its tokens leave the window.” A matched plan-only removal condition is missing. So are irrelevant-plan removal, corrupted-plan replacement, current-state-only reconstruction, rereading/re-observation, incremental budget sweeps, and plan-plus-state summaries. `plan_protected` also needs an exact capacity ledger: pinning an extra plan while retaining four messages is not obviously the same token budget as naive truncation, and the paper reports neither visible-token distributions nor which messages/spans were displaced.

The probe-gated policy re-surfaces the same stale static plan 6.1 times per run. It does not restore evicted observations, reconcile completed subgoals, or generate a state-conditioned residual plan. Its failure therefore does not falsify all adaptive context policies; it falsifies this monitor-and-static-plan package at one severe budget.

## Unique insight for skill-bench

The paper's most useful lesson is not “plans disappear.” It is that **context interventions require a proposition-and-consequence ledger, because textual removal, representational sensitivity, behavioral adoption, and endpoint effect are different experiments**.

A valid chain is:

```text
versioned plan proposition / obligation
→ source span and authority
→ decision-time visible context and derived restatements
→ matched removal / corruption / irrelevant / restoration treatment
→ proposition-specific representation or recoverability observation
→ freely chosen action distribution and obligation adoption
→ realized artifact/state consequence
→ cost and context-policy reliability
→ bounded transport/readiness claim
```

Replay pairing observes a broad history perturbation at the representation rung. Forced replay deliberately blocks the action and consequence rungs. The compression stress test observes endpoint consequence under a broad context-budget treatment, but does not isolate the plan rung. Joining their directionally compatible results does not fill the missing causal links.

This produces two cross-domain benchmark requirements:

1. **Derived-trace treatment contamination:** removing an original requirement is insufficient when summaries, scratchpads, reasoning traces, action rationales, or tool outputs restate it. Every condition needs an information-flow audit over all agent-visible derivatives.
2. **State-conditioned obligation validity:** a useful plan is not a static paragraph. Each clause should be linked to prerequisites, completion state, supersession, acceptable alternatives, and later actions. “Plan present” and “plan cosine signal” are too coarse to diagnose whether the currently live obligation survived.

## Comparison with adjacent reviewed evidence

- **ACON** shows that task success can coexist with corrupted state and that compression bundles reset, reformatting, recency, and omission. This paper provides a cleaner forced-prefix representation contrast, but still deletes a broad exchange and lacks fidelity labels. Together they require raw-state preservation plus typed transformation and consequence checks.
- **Governance Decay** more directly removes and restores a named constraint and observes a downstream proposed action, though its source authority and release are weak. Plans Don't Persist has stronger hidden-state instrumentation but no proposition-specific removal/restoration or freely chosen downstream action in the paired experiment.
- **Decision Fidelity under Context Compression** compares downstream response distributions under source and compressed views. That evaluator-relative decision rung is still stronger than cosine distance for behavioral relevance, while neither establishes independent correctness without a separate warrant.
- **PM-Bench** makes obligation activation, update, cue access, timing, lures, and selected action explicit. Plans Don't Persist probes a coarse plan block but does not represent live versus completed clauses. Combining the methods would require proposition-level obligations plus a graded concurrent artifact, not a new memory scalar.
- **Configured-system/context-state machinery** already has homes for context-policy identity, compression events, evidence views, traces, obligations, artifact/state consequences, metrics, and validity claims. No plan-persistence-specific schema is warranted.

## Evidence and claim boundaries

### Supported

1. Removing the full guard-plan exchange changes final-token hidden states strongly on the next forced call and less after later forced actions/observations in the reported Llama ALFWorld and HotpotQA trajectories.
2. The history perturbation has a mid-layer peak and a broadly similar descriptive shape across six ALFWorld task labels and two Llama scales.
3. Prior reasoning traces can reintroduce treatment-related information into a nominally stripped replay; broad strict stripping increases the measured contrast in the small reported R1 sample.
4. Step/phase is strongly decodable and confounds the probe; the authors disclose this directly.
5. A severe four-recent-message policy lowers ALFWorld success substantially relative to full context in the reported 30-task repeated study.
6. Retaining or conditionally re-injecting the original static plan does not repair that severe broad-history truncation.
7. Uniform or head-aware intervention along the reported linear contrast is not a reliable behavioral control in the tested sweeps.

### Partially supported

- **Cross-domain representation:** similar initial perturbation and probe ranking appear in HotpotQA, but position/discourse, task phase, and content are not separated.
- **Reasoning-trace correction:** strict stripping reveals contamination, but asymmetric whole-block removal does not identify the plan-specific component.
- **Early warning:** the probe precedes one judge-defined deviation on selected trajectories, but phase leakage, task dependence, threshold ambiguity, and absent judge validation block an operational detector claim.
- **Context-management sensitivity:** endpoint success depends on retained history, but the missing plan-only contrast prevents attribution to plan eviction.

### Not supported

- proposition-level plan encoding, persistence, forgetting, or recovery;
- internal recurrent state decay across model calls;
- causal linkage from cosine-distance decay to later free actions or task failure;
- content-specific probe validity or architecture-invariant plan direction;
- a reliable plan-loss detector or effective re-surfacing policy;
- general context-policy reliability, memory capability, or planning ability;
- professional knowledge-work validity, production fitness, safety, or readiness.

## Limitations and validity threats

1. The treatment removes a guard-plus-plan exchange, not plan content alone.
2. History length, token position, turn count, discourse, and attention/cache geometry differ.
3. Forced replay discards B's outputs and therefore cannot observe behavioral adoption.
4. No clause-level plan representation, source locator, prerequisite, completion, update, contradiction, or alternative-path label exists.
5. No filler, irrelevant-plan, corrupted-plan, shuffled-plan, paraphrase, clause-ablation, or matched-position controls are run.
6. Last-token cosine distance is not independently validated as plan information or recoverability.
7. A shrinking representation difference is described as hidden-state persistence despite stateless recomputation from history across calls.
8. Step index is nearly perfectly decodable and closely coupled to the target.
9. Cross-validation appears row-stratified rather than grouped by task, risking trajectory leakage.
10. The stated 50-train/30-held-out split is not reconciled with five-fold cross-validation over all 80 tasks.
11. Variable trajectory length and per-offset survivor/termination denominators are not reported.
12. Probe positives, “fire,” and low-signal intervention triggering use potentially inconsistent semantics.
13. The lag endpoint uses one uncalibrated model judge and an authored 0.3 threshold.
14. Calibration on an easy phase-defined contrast does not validate intervention decisions.
15. Strict stripping is asymmetric and removes all prior reasoning, not plan spans.
16. Five in-sample and five held-out R1 tasks are too small for broad reasoning-model claims.
17. Self-probe `n=85` and Qwen `n=270` are nested step records, not independent tasks.
18. Cross-model behavioral cells use unequal run counts and different decoding regimes.
19. Phase-1 raw/strict trajectories are not replay-paired and complete-case matching can select comparable prefixes post treatment.
20. The raw-log success field is known to be wrong for goal completion; the corrected analysis is unreleased.
21. Residual steering does not causally validate the measurement and can destabilize generation.
22. The compression stress test removes almost all working history, not only the plan.
23. Plan-protected budget equivalence is not demonstrated with token/message distributions.
24. Only one severe `keep_recent=4` budget is tested; no response curve identifies a threshold.
25. Static plan restoration ignores changed state, completed subgoals, corrections, and supersession.
26. Repeated runs are clustered by 30 tasks; exact seeds, run pairing, and per-row outcomes are absent.
27. Models are largely Llama-derived, environments are ALFWorld/HotpotQA, and no professional artifact workflow is studied.
28. No code, prompts, trajectories, hidden states, manifests, or analysis release enables replay.
29. Approximately 156 reported H200-hours—and 450–500 including exploration—make independent reproduction expensive even if artifacts were released.

## Reproducibility and operational realism

**Conceptual reproducibility is moderate.** The paper specifies models, ReAct setup, guard text, plan injection steps, replay formula, sampled layers, decoding settings, probe families, thresholds, task counts, compute, compression policies, task-success definition, and principal statistical tests. The pre-plan identity check and exact explanation of strict stripping are especially useful.

**Result reproducibility is poor.** There is no official release of task IDs, generated plans, trajectories, prompts, hidden arrays, folds, seeds, judge records, policy views, outputs, or scripts. Mutable model-serving stacks and 8×H200 requirements compound the problem. Several important analysis identities are ambiguous: 50/30 versus all-80 probe use, step-record versus task unit, per-offset survivor sets, strict-strip transfer `n`, and plan-protected capacity.

**Operational realism is low to moderate.** ALFWorld provides interactive state and repeated actions; the compression study uses actual free agent runs and a realistic finite-context pressure. But the plan is elicited by a synthetic guard, B is a forced observer replay, HotpotQA is another controlled benchmark, and the context policy is a very aggressive recent-four truncation. There are no professional artifacts, stakeholders, permissions, evolving authoritative evidence, handoffs, irreversible actions, or real context-manager incidents.

## Transfer to skill-bench

### Retain

1. **Forced-prefix replay as an observer tool.** Use it to isolate representation or evaluator-view differences while explicitly labeling that it blocks behavioral divergence.
2. **Pre-treatment identity checks.** Verify zero difference before intervention and preserve exact context hashes at every call.
3. **Derived-trace contamination audits.** Search summaries, thoughts, action rationales, tool outputs, memories, and scratchpads for treatment propositions before calling a condition absent.
4. **Compactor/actor separation.** Record context operator, actor, visible view, generated derivatives, and sampled outputs independently.
5. **Matched task-level repeated policy tests.** Retain task pairing, all attempts, invalids, and cluster-aware uncertainty.
6. **Null interventions.** A detectable direction that cannot reliably move behavior should remain a diagnostic observer, not be promoted to a control mechanism.

### Repair

1. **Atomize the plan.** Represent each obligation with source span, authority, prerequisites, live/completed/superseded state, acceptable alternatives, required evidence, and downstream action/check IDs.
2. **Cross content controls at matched capacity.** Compare exact plan, paraphrase, length-matched neutral text, irrelevant valid plan, corrupted clause, omitted clause, contradiction, and restored clause while matching positions as closely as possible.
3. **Add free-action twins.** Pair forced replay for representation with separately seeded free continuations from the same frozen prefix; measure action distributions, obligation adoption, artifact/state consequences, and divergence.
4. **Use restoration and re-observation controls.** Restore one proposition, current state, recent observations, or both. This distinguishes plan loss from working-state loss and tests whether stale plans help.
5. **Group statistics by task/trajectory.** Keep steps nested, split by task, report per-offset at-risk counts, and predeclare the task-level estimand.
6. **Separate observer targets.** Keep text retention, proposition recoverability, representation sensitivity, behavioral adoption, endpoint consequence, efficiency, and decision utility as different outcomes.
7. **Sweep budgets and policies.** Compare plan-only removal, recent-state-only, state summary, protected live obligations, re-observation, and full context over multiple capacities with exact visible-token ledgers.
8. **Validate judges and thresholds.** Use proposition-specific expert or deterministic checks where possible; retain judge identity, evidence view, repeated labels, uncertainty, and decision loss.

## Concrete repository actions

No new queue task is warranted. The completed context-compression fidelity slice, delayed-obligation pilots, configured-system identity, trace/evidence-view, artifact/state consequence, metric, task-health, and validity contracts already provide nonduplicate homes for these requirements.

For the next real or independently reviewed longitudinal pilot, the existing compression slice should be calibrated with one **plan-obligation contrast** rather than expanded into a new subsystem: exact live obligation versus matched irrelevant/corrupted/omitted/restored variants; forced observer replay plus free-action twins; current-state-only and plan-plus-state restoration; and separate proposition recovery, action adoption, artifact consequence, and cost outcomes. This is a future calibration condition, not a new queue item.

## Bottom line

*Plans Don't Persist* contributes a valuable warning and a useful method: removing a source span is not enough when derived reasoning traces reintroduce it, and aggressive context truncation can destroy agent performance. Its own evidence also imposes a stricter interpretation than the title. The primary experiment measures how the final-token representation changes when an entire plan exchange is absent from a forced history; it neither observes a recurrent state decaying nor lets the stripped condition choose actions. The endpoint experiment then deletes most working state, and the failure of static-plan protection shows that plan text is not the isolated cause. For `skill-bench`, the durable lesson is a claim ladder and an experimental repair: trace each plan obligation through visibility, derived restatements, proposition-specific representation, free action, and realized consequence, and never infer the missing rungs from cosine distance plus a separately confounded success gap.
